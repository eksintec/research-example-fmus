Usage of the model¶

The model takes the current power \(p\) consumed by the plant as an input. You have to provide this value by some external measurement or estimation. Together with the mix factor \(c_\text{Mix}\), the FMU can estimate the carbon emissions carried out.

In a first step, the emission flow \(\dot m_{CO_2}\) is calculated: this is the amount of carbon dioxide emitted per second caused by the plant operation.

By integration of both \(p\) and \(\dot m_{CO_2}\), the energy and the carbon emissions can be evaluated.

\[E_\text{Tot} = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} p \, dt \qquad m_{CO_2} = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} \dot m_{CO_2} \, dt\]

The time span \([t_1,t_2]\) must be defined in order to evaluate the integrals. In the practical implementation, there are the signals meas_start and meas_stop. Upon a positive edge of these, the internal structures assume the current time as \(t_1\) or \(t_2\) respectively.

For convenient usage, the current simulation time is stored in measurement_t_start or measurement_t_stop for later evaluation. The flags measurement_started and measurement_stopped allow to quickly elaborate the current state of the measurement: initially, both are false. Upon positive edges of the meas_* signals, the corresponding time stamp is stored and the corresponding flag indicates a successful transition.

To allow for multiple measurements, especially in virtual commissioning, a rest is implemented. The reset is triggered by means of a positive edge on meas_reset. All internal integration is reset, the time span removed (the validity flags and the time stamps).

Please note: Restarting the measurement is not possible without prior reset of the states.
