Usage of the model
==================

The model takes the current power :math:`p` consumed by the plant as an input.
You have to provide this value by some external measurement or estimation.
Together with the mix factor :math:`c_\text{Mix}`, the FMU can estimate the carbon emissions carried out.

In a first step, the emission flow :math:`\dot m_{CO_2}` is calculated:
this is the amount of carbon dioxide emitted per second caused by the plant operation.

By integration of both :math:`p` and :math:`\dot m_{CO_2}`, the energy and the carbon emissions can be evaluated.

.. math::
    
    E_\text{Tot} = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} p \, dt  \qquad    m_{CO_2} = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} \dot m_{CO_2} \, dt

The time span :math:`[t_1,t_2]` must be defined in order to evaluate the integrals.
In the practical implementation, there are the signals ``meas_start`` and ``meas_stop``.
Upon a positive edge of these, the internal structures assume the current time as :math:`t_1` or :math:`t_2` respectively.

For convenient usage, the current simulation time is stored in ``measurement_t_start`` or ``measurement_t_stop`` for later evaluation.
The flags ``measurement_started`` and ``measurement_stopped`` allow to quickly elaborate the current state of the measurement:
initially, both are ``false``.
Upon positive edges of the ``meas_*`` signals, the corresponding time stamp is stored and the corresponding flag indicates a successful transition.

To allow for multiple measurements, especially in virtual commissioning, a rest is implemented.
The reset is triggered automatically by starting the measurement.
No manual reset is required.
All internal integration is reset, the time span removed (the validity flags and the time stamps).
